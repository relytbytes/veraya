What it is. It’s called Veraya. The short version is that it’s a full operating system for restaurants. The register, the kitchen and bar screens, reservations and the host stand, inventory, purchasing, payroll, scheduling, all of it. It runs on the web and as a native mobile app, and it’s live right now at veraya.vercel.app if you want to poke around while you read this.

Why it’s different. The part I’m actually excited about isn’t the restaurant plumbing. It’s a layer on top that I named Vera. Most of these systems just record what already happened. Vera predicts. It forecasts each day, projects the restaurant’s profit and loss in real time, and tells the operator how much to prep and what to reorder before they run short. And it learns each restaurant individually. It watches how every day actually plays out and quietly adjusts itself overnight to figure out what really drives that one venue’s numbers. Nobody big in this space leads with that, and I think it’s the whole reason there could be a real company here.

The piece left to build is the shared layer that runs a lot of them from one place, with central accounts and billing, which is what turns a strong product into an efficient business at scale.
