# Veraya — Proprietary Technology

*One-page overview of Veraya's differentiating technology. Plain-English; for decks, investor diligence, and counsel.*

---

## The core asset: Vera, a self-learning operations brain

Most restaurant software *records* what happened. Veraya **predicts what's about to happen and tells the operator what to do about it** — and it gets smarter at each individual venue the longer it runs.

That intelligence comes from **Vera**, a closed learning loop with no equivalent in mainstream POS or reservation platforms:

> **The forecast** predicts the day → **the nightly snapshot** records how it actually went → **the model re-weights itself** to learn which signals predict *this* restaurant's profit → **staff feedback** tunes what gets surfaced → the next forecast is sharper.

Every service makes the system more accurate for that specific location. A competitor can copy a screen; they cannot copy the months of per-venue learning baked into a live deployment. **That compounding accuracy is the moat.**

---

## Proprietary capabilities

**1. Vera Health Engine.** Continuously scores the business across five live dimensions — profitability, demand, labor, cost, and service — blends them by how confident each signal is, and projects the full day's P&L by fusing real-time pace with the forecast. Answers "are we going to make money today, and why or why not?" in real time.

**2. Research-grade demand forecasting.** A purpose-built model: recency-weighted seasonal patterns, damped trend, reservations and events, weather, holidays, and a **newsvendor prep optimizer** that converts the forecast into how much to actually prep — broken down by daypart, with honest confidence bands.

**3. Self-tuning.** The forecast continuously back-tests itself against its own history and re-tunes its parameters automatically — no data scientist required.

**4. Per-venue learned baselines.** Vera learns each location's day-of-week rhythm and its hour-by-hour sales curve, so "ahead or behind pace" is specific to *your* restaurant, not a generic benchmark.

**5. Learned signal weighting.** The system measures which factors actually predict *this* venue's profit and weights its diagnosis accordingly — it discovers, for example, that weather matters more to a patio concept than a steakhouse, on its own.

**6. Weather- and calendar-aware demand.** Live weather and holiday signals are folded directly into the forecast and prep, with a forward-looking demand-impact read on the week ahead.

**7. AI invoice & label intelligence.** Photograph a supplier invoice and Veraya reads the vendor, every line item, and its cost — auto-building the supplier database and updating ingredient costs. Vision recognition identifies specific products (down to the exact wine and vintage), not just barcodes.

**8. Predictive purchasing.** Forecast-driven reorder suggestions and run-out prediction tell operators what to buy and when, before they run short.

**9. Service-stage inference.** Veraya reads each table's order to automatically infer where the party is in its meal — appetizers, entrées, dessert, check — and advances the floor view without staff input.

**10. Guest recognition & insight.** Automatically identifies regulars and VIPs from visit patterns and surfaces each guest's favorites, typical spend, and dwell time to the team before they arrive.

**11. Intelligent floor & labor operations.** Cover-aware pacing limits, balanced server assignment, auto-scheduling against forecasted demand, and overtime-aware labor projection.

## How it's protected

- **Trade secret + copyright** cover the algorithms and — most importantly — the *way they compose* into the self-learning loop, plus the accumulated per-venue model data.
- The **self-tuning forecast method** and the **multi-dimensional, confidence-weighted health-scoring method** are the strongest candidates for a **provisional patent** if formal protection is desired.
- The data moat is operational: value accrues to whoever has the most live, learning deployments.
